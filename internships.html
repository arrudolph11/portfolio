2020 Internship: R&D Clinical Development Intern @ Horizon Therapeutics

As I was tasked with validating these low activity levels, I immediately focused on numerical measurements through electronic capture: measurements from wearable accelerometer devices. Such computerized measurements are what came to mind when I thought of data analysis. However as I immersed myself in FDA guidelines on the use of such devices and learned about their limitations (such as lack of technical training and limited access to monitors), I realized that one needs much more information to understand the full story of a patient’s activity reduction.

In addition to the accelerometer, I added the 6 Minute Walk Test (6MWT) to my study. It’s an important, validated measurement that assesses aerobic capacity. However, even with an additional form of measurement, I sensed that I still wasn’t capturing the patient’s full story: The 6MWT only measures activity in one setting in a single point in time.

To further understand the patient perspective, I created a questionnaire which asked patients to check off a list of comorbid illnesses and rank how those affect their activity in relation to ILD. Rather than drawing a conclusion based on a few measurements, this questionnaire was a valuable tool in gaining insight on the patient perspective, quality of life, and other factors that may reduce activity.
